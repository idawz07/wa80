Updated sequence

WA80-8 field sequence

  1. Opening and machine identification
    Wide shot, machine tag, work area.
  2. Pre-start safety and cleanliness
    Fuel hoses, clamps, guards, cushions, nearby wiring, spilled fuel, flammable debris.
  3. Fuel spray prevention caps check
    Confirm all caps are installed correctly on injector pipe ends.
  4. Open right-side engine cover
    Show service access for the fuel system.
  5. Bleed air from fuel circuit
    Use the main filter air bleed plug and feed pump until fuel is free of bubbles.
  6. Final pre-start fuel inspection
    Filter area, injector piping area, disturbed joints.
  7. Engine start
    Show operator action and instrument panel if possible.
  8. Leak test stage 1: low idle
    Run at low idle, stop engine, inspect for leaks.
  9. Leak test stage 2: high idle
    Run at high idle, stop engine, inspect again.
  10. Leak test stage 3: load / throttle
    Apply load or controlled raised-rpm condition, stop engine, inspect again.
  11. Final warning and response check
    No abnormal warnings, smooth response.
  12. Closing confirmation
    Machine running normally, clean final shot.
Critical pass conditions

Do not proceed unless these are true

  • Fuel tank has been filled before bleeding.
  • No spilled fuel, open flame, smoking, or flammable debris near the machine.
  • No loose clamps, guards, cushions, connectors, or wiring near the serviced area.
  • Fuel spray prevention caps are installed after injector pipe installation.
  • Fuel bleeding is completed until no air bubbles appear.
  • Bleed plug is tightened correctly.
  • Feed pump knob is pushed back in and tightened.
  • No fuel leakage is visible at low idle, high idle, or load check.
  • No abnormal warning lights are shown after testing.
Bleed plug torque
7.84 to 9.8 Nm
Injector pipe sleeve nuts
39 to 44 Nm
Injector holder bolt
39 to 49 Nm
Key update from manual

3-stage fuel leakage examination

Best-practice test after injector installation: clean the engine and sprayed areas first, apply developer / dye to the supply pump, common rail, injectors, and fuel pipes, then inspect after each stage. If leakage is found at any stage, repair it and restart the procedure.

Stage 1: Run at low idle until engine speed becomes stable, stop engine, inspect sprayed areas.
Stage 2: Run at high idle, stop engine, inspect again.
Stage 3: Relieve pressure, apply load to the engine, stop engine, inspect again.
Important: The post-injector check is not just “start and look.” The manual uses a run-stop-inspect sequence at multiple conditions.

Before start

Inspection points to use

  • Fuel tank filled.
  • No spilled fuel.
  • No open flame or smoking nearby.
  • No flammable debris around engine area.
  • No loose hose/tube clamps, guards, or cushions.
  • No loose or damaged connectors and wiring near the serviced area.
  • No foreign material left around the fuel system.
  • Fuel spray prevention caps installed on the injector pipe ends.
  • Fuel injection pipes not bent or visibly damaged.
  • Bleeding completed until fuel is free of bubbles.
  • Bleed plug tightened.
  • Feed pump knob pushed back in and tightened.
After start

Inspection points to confirm

  • Engine starts normally.
  • Stable idle.
  • No abnormal warning lights.
  • No fuel leakage at supply pump, common rail, injectors, fuel pipes, main filter, bleed plug, or disturbed joints.
  • No abnormal smoke.
  • No abnormal vibration or noise.
  • Smooth rpm response.
  • Passes low idle, high idle, and load leak inspections.

Field reminders

Notes for filming

  • Film each critical step twice when possible: one wide shot and one close-up.
  • Use portable lighting for filter, injector pipe, and common rail close-ups.
  • Move slowly during leak checks so dry surfaces are visible on camera.
  • Use the run-stop-inspect method, not just a quick visual while running.
  • If leakage is found at any stage, repair it and restart the inspection sequence.
